Project Overview

Traditional ITGC audits rely heavily on manual sampling, static checklists, and individual auditor judgment. As IT environments scale, this approach becomes time-consuming, inconsistent, and difficult to standardize.

This platform was designed to replicate real-world IT audit workflows while augmenting auditor judgment through responsible AI usage, without compromising control integrity or audit independence.

Problem Statement

Manual sampling limits audit coverage and scalability
Risk assessment varies across auditors
Large ITGC datasets are difficult to review holistically
Traditional tools lack adaptability to data quality issues

Solution Overview

Modular ITGC Workflows

Structured audit modules aligned to real ITGC domains with standardized testing procedures.

AI-Assisted Risk Assessment

Contextual risk classification to support auditor judgment with explainable outputs.

Intelligent Sampling Engine

Stratified, weighted, and risk-based sampling for comprehensive audit coverage.

Audit-Ready Outputs

Clear samples, observations, and documentation for seamless audit review.

Core ITGC Modules

Change Management

  • AI-based risk classification (High / Medium / Low)
  • Critical change prioritization and flagging
  • Risk-weighted sampling logic
  • Automated audit summaries and narratives

Incident Management

  • SLA compliance validation and breach detection
  • Start-to-Resolve and Resolve-to-Close analysis
  • Priority-based incident sampling
  • Threshold breach identification and reporting

User Access Management

  • HR vs AD vs Access reconciliation
  • Dormant user identification and analysis
  • Status-based access analysis
  • Potential segregation-of-duties risks

Role of AI in the Platform

AI is used to augment the audit process â€” not replace it. The platform maintains human oversight and audit judgment at all critical decision points.

  • Risk classification and prioritization based on contextual analysis
  • Audit insight summarization for faster review
  • Drafting of observation narratives with auditor approval

AI does not override controls, make final audit decisions, or perform sampling without predefined audit rules and auditor oversight.
